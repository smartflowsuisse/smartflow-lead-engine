# SmartFlow Suisse — Client Audit Checklist Draft

## 1. Purpose

This checklist is used before proposing any automation or workflow implementation.

Goal:

- understand how the client receives and handles requests;
- identify where time is lost;
- find 1 or 2 realistic quick wins;
- avoid proposing complex tools too early;
- prepare a simple mini-audit for the client.

## 2. Basic company information

Collect:

- company name;
- website;
- contact email;
- phone;
- city/canton;
- industry;
- main services;
- decision-maker name if known;
- current tools if known.

## 3. Website and contact process

Check:

- website works on desktop;
- website works on mobile;
- contact form exists;
- email is visible;
- phone is visible;
- contact page is easy to find;
- legal/impressum page exists if relevant;
- call-to-action is clear;
- form confirmation is clear;
- form sends to a real inbox;
- response expectation is visible.

Notes:

- do not over-focus on SEO;
- use website audit as entry point to discuss lost requests and follow-up process.

## 4. Incoming request workflow

Ask or verify:

- where new requests arrive;
- email inbox;
- website form;
- phone;
- WhatsApp or SMS;
- PDF documents;
- quote requests;
- referrals;
- walk-ins.

Questions:

- who checks new requests?
- how quickly are they answered?
- where are they recorded?
- what happens if nobody replies the same day?
- are requests ever lost?
- are old requests easy to find?

## 5. Follow-up workflow

Check:

- does the company track follow-ups?
- is there a clear New / Contacted / Follow-up / Won / Lost status?
- who is responsible for follow-up?
- are reminders used?
- are follow-ups manual or automated?
- are missed follow-ups common?
- is there a simple overview of open opportunities?

Potential quick win:

- simple Google Sheets CRM;
- follow-up date column;
- automatic reminder;
- weekly open-request summary.

## 6. Document workflow

Check if the client handles:

- PDFs;
- quotes;
- invoices;
- photos;
- plans;
- contracts;
- supplier documents;
- job reports;
- client emails with attachments.

Questions:

- are documents renamed manually?
- are PDFs copied into folders?
- are quote details retyped?
- are documents sent to accountant/admin manually?
- are documents easy to retrieve?

Potential quick win:

- incoming document checklist;
- PDF/email extraction draft;
- structured Google Drive folders;
- AI draft summary for human review.

## 7. Repetitive admin tasks

Look for repeated tasks:

- copying data from emails;
- writing similar replies;
- creating quote follow-ups;
- chasing missing documents;
- sending reminders;
- updating spreadsheets;
- preparing weekly status;
- checking form submissions;
- summarizing client requests.

Question:

> Which task do you repeat every week that feels simple but wastes time?

## 8. Current tools

Identify current tools:

- Gmail / Outlook;
- Excel / Google Sheets;
- Google Drive / OneDrive;
- CRM;
- accounting software;
- website CMS;
- calendar;
- WhatsApp / phone;
- PDF tools.

Rule:

Do not propose replacing tools immediately.

First propose connecting or simplifying what already exists.

## 9. Data-safety check

Before any implementation, confirm:

- what data is sensitive;
- whether client data may be used in AI tools;
- who approves access;
- who can view the CRM/sheet;
- whether backups are needed;
- whether automated sending is allowed;
- whether human approval is required before messages.

Default rule:

- no automated client-facing sending without explicit approval;
- no sensitive data sent to AI without review.

## 10. Automation opportunity scoring

Score each opportunity from 1 to 5:

### Time saved

1 = small  
5 = high weekly time saving

### Simplicity

1 = complex  
5 = easy to implement

### Risk

1 = risky  
5 = low-risk

### Client clarity

1 = hard to explain  
5 = easy for client to understand

Best first quick win:

- high time saved;
- simple;
- low-risk;
- easy to explain.

## 11. Recommended quick-win types

Good first quick wins:

1. Contact form → Google Sheets CRM.
2. Email/request → structured CRM row.
3. New request → notification.
4. Lead/request → follow-up task.
5. AI draft reply for human review.
6. Weekly summary of open requests.
7. PDF/email summary for admin review.
8. Simple dashboard of New / Contacted / Follow-up / Won / Lost.

Avoid first:

- full CRM migration;
- automated mass emails;
- complex ERP integration;
- full website rebuild unless needed;
- public SaaS;
- large AI platform.

## 12. Mini-audit output

The client-facing mini-audit should include:

- company name;
- current observed workflow;
- 2 or 3 issues;
- 1 or 2 quick wins;
- recommended first step;
- estimated complexity;
- safety notes;
- next action.

## 13. Internal decision after audit

After audit, choose one path:

### Path A — No fit

Reason:

- no clear pain;
- no reachable decision-maker;
- weak data;
- too complex for first project.

### Path B — Free audit only

Use when:

- client may be interesting but not ready;
- keep relationship open.

### Path C — Quick Win proposal

Use when:

- clear repetitive task;
- easy automation;
- low risk;
- client can understand value.

### Path D — Website + Workflow proposal

Use when:

- website/contact process is weak;
- lead capture is poor;
- workflow needs basic structure.

## 14. Internal checklist before proposal

Before sending any proposal:

- confirm client problem;
- confirm one recommended quick win;
- confirm price range;
- confirm required tools/access;
- confirm data-safety limits;
- prepare short PDF;
- prepare next step;
- human review before sending.
