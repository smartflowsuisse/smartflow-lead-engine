# SmartFlow Client Demo Checklist

## Purpose

This checklist is for preparing and running a safe first-client demo.

The goal is to show SmartFlow's value clearly without overpromising, exposing sensitive data, or enabling automatic external actions.

## Demo scope

The demo may show:

- how a lead or request enters the workflow;
- how data is stored in a CRM-style table;
- how AI can summarize the request;
- how SmartFlow prepares a draft next step;
- how a human reviews and approves the action;
- how follow-up can be tracked manually.

The demo must stay simple, practical, and business-focused.

## Before the demo

### 1. Confirm demo account and access

Verify:

- Google Form is accessible through the SmartFlow account;
- Google Sheet is accessible through the SmartFlow account;
- Make scenario uses the correct SmartFlow connections;
- demo files do not belong only to a personal account;
- scenario scheduling is off unless explicitly needed;
- no automatic email sending is enabled.

Preferred SmartFlow account:

- smartflowsuisse@gmail.com

### 2. Confirm demo data

Use fake or low-risk demo data by default.

Check:

- company name is safe to show;
- contact email is fake or approved for demo;
- phone number is fake or approved for demo;
- website is safe to show;
- no private client information is exposed;
- no real client data is shown without permission.

### 3. Confirm scenario safety

Before running any scenario, verify:

- no automatic external email is sent;
- no mass outreach is triggered;
- no real lead is contacted automatically;
- any Gmail module, if present, creates a draft only and does not send;
- the Make scenario is run manually for the demo;
- the output goes to a safe internal place.

### 4. Confirm demo narrative

Prepare a short explanation:

- what business problem this solves;
- what manual work it reduces;
- where AI helps;
- where human review stays in control;
- what the client would receive after setup.

Keep the explanation practical.

Avoid technical overload.

## During the demo

### Step 1 — Show intake

Show how a request or lead enters the system.

Example:

- Google Form submission;
- manual CRM entry;
- imported lead;
- website contact request.

Explain that the intake method can be adapted to the client's current workflow.

### Step 2 — Show CRM structure

Show the CRM-style table or Lead Engine view.

Point out:

- company;
- contact person;
- source;
- language;
- status;
- priority;
- notes;
- next action.

Do not expose private or unrelated leads.

### Step 3 — Show AI summary

Show how AI summarizes the request.

The summary should help answer:

- what the client needs;
- what the likely problem is;
- what service may help;
- what information is missing;
- what the next step should be.

Clarify that AI output is a draft, not an automatic decision.

### Step 4 — Show manual review

Explain that SmartFlow keeps a human approval step.

Before any action, check:

- is the data correct;
- is the summary accurate;
- is the suggested action appropriate;
- is the email address verified;
- is the message safe to send.

### Step 5 — Show next action

Allowed demo outputs:

- update CRM status;
- create a note;
- prepare a draft reply;
- create a follow-up task;
- show a manual action recommendation.

Not allowed in the demo unless explicitly approved:

- automatic external sending;
- mass outreach;
- contacting real leads;
- changing production systems;
- using sensitive client data.

## After the demo

Record:

- client name;
- date of demo;
- what was shown;
- client reaction;
- main pain points;
- requested features;
- promised next step;
- follow-up date;
- risks or open questions.

## Client questions to ask

Ask simple discovery questions:

- How do new requests arrive today?
- Where do you currently track leads or clients?
- Who answers first client messages?
- What usually gets delayed or forgotten?
- Which tasks are repetitive every week?
- Do you already use Google Sheets, Excel, CRM, or email templates?
- Would you prefer a simple internal tool or a more automated workflow?
- Who must approve outgoing messages?
- What data must stay private?
- What would make this useful within the first month?

## Safety rules

### No automatic sending

Do not send emails automatically during the demo.

### No mass outreach

Do not use the demo for bulk outreach.

### Human approval required

Any external message must be reviewed and approved manually.

### No sensitive data exposure

Do not show private, financial, medical, legal, or confidential client data unless explicitly approved and necessary.

### No production changes

Do not change production systems during a demo.

### No overpromising

Do not promise integrations, timelines, or savings that were not verified.

## Good demo outcome

A good demo is successful if the client understands:

- what problem SmartFlow solves;
- what part is automated;
- what part stays human-controlled;
- what setup would look like;
- what the next safe step is.

## Recommended next step after demo

Offer one clear next step:

- a paid workflow audit;
- a small automation quick win;
- a CRM cleanup;
- a website/contact-form improvement;
- a follow-up proposal.

Do not push a large project before the client's actual workflow is understood.
