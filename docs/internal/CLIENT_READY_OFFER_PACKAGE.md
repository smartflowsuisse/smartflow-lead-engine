# Client-Ready Offer Package

## Purpose

This document defines the first client-ready commercial offer package for SmartFlow Suisse.

It translates the completed MVP2 Client Hub preview/demo layer into a clear business offer that can be explained during a first client conversation.

This offer is designed for a controlled pilot implementation.

---

## 1. Problem

Many small and medium businesses receive client requests through several disconnected channels:

- website forms;
- email;
- phone notes;
- spreadsheets;
- manual follow-up lists;
- internal messages.

This creates operational problems:

- requests are difficult to track;
- follow-up depends on memory;
- urgent leads may be missed;
- client information is scattered;
- manual work takes too much time;
- there is no clear approval process before action;
- business owners cannot easily see what should happen next.

The result is slower response time, lost opportunities, and unnecessary administrative work.

---

## 2. Solution

SmartFlow Suisse provides a controlled Lead & Follow-up workflow for small and medium businesses.

The system helps structure incoming requests, summarize them, prepare follow-up, and preview client conversion before any irreversible action.

Core principle:

The system prepares the work. The human approves the action.

The workflow is designed to support the team, not replace human judgment.

---

## 3. Demo Workflow

The demo workflow is:

Request
→ Lead Engine
→ Client Hub
→ AI-assisted summary
→ Manual follow-up preparation
→ Convert to Client preview
→ Human approval

### What the demo shows

The demo shows how a client request can become a structured business workflow.

It explains how the business can:

- capture incoming requests;
- organize lead information;
- understand priority and next action;
- prepare follow-up faster;
- preview what would happen before client creation;
- keep approval under human control.

### What the demo does not do

The demo does not perform real production conversion.

It does not automatically send external messages.

It does not change client data without approval.

## 5. Timeline

A controlled pilot can usually be structured in phases.

### Phase 1 — Discovery and process mapping

Estimated duration: 1 to 3 days.

Goal:

Understand the client's current request and follow-up process.

### Phase 2 — Demo workflow setup

Estimated duration: 3 to 7 days.

Goal:

Prepare a controlled workflow using one intake process.

### Phase 3 — Review and adjustment

Estimated duration: 2 to 5 days.

Goal:

Test the workflow with realistic examples and adjust fields, wording, and approvals.

### Phase 4 — Pilot handover

Estimated duration: 1 to 3 days.

Goal:

Give the client a clear operating process and define next steps.

Total estimated pilot timeline:

7 to 21 days depending on complexity, responsiveness, and available client data.

## 6. Price Range

Recommended first pilot range:

CHF 1,500 to CHF 4,500

The final price depends on:

- number of intake channels;
- complexity of the process;
- number of languages;
- existing tools used by the client;
- required documentation;
- level of customization;
- whether website/form changes are included.

For a very simple first workflow, the pilot can start at the lower end.

For a more complete workflow with Client Hub demo, AI summary, follow-up preparation, and documented approval process, the price should be positioned closer to the middle or upper part of the range.

## 8. What Is Excluded

The first pilot does not include:

- full CRM replacement;

- legal compliance automation;
- automatic external email campaigns;
- mass outreach;
- WhatsApp automation;
- Telegram automation;

- AI agent with autonomous actions;
- production client creation without approval;
- database migration unless agreed separately;
- long-term maintenance unless covered by a care plan.

---

## 9. Safety / Approval Policy

SmartFlow Suisse uses a human-approval-first approach.

During the pilot:

- no email is sent automatically;
- no client is created automatically;
- no lead status is changed without review;
- no production automation is enabled without explicit approval;
- no mass outreach is included;
- no external system action is performed without validation.

This protects the client from accidental communication, incorrect data changes, and operational risk.

---

## 10. Client Requirements

To start a pilot, the client should provide:

- description of the current request process;
- examples of typical client requests;
- current follow-up method;
- list of tools currently used;
- preferred language for client communication;
- one responsible contact person;
- approval for a controlled demo/pilot scope.

The pilot should start with one clear process, not the entire business.

---

## 11. Recommended First Pilot Scope

Recommended first scope:

This keeps the first implementation safe, understandable, and useful.
