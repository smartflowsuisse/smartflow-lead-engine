# SmartFlow Lead Engine - Client Reply Playbook

Internal guide for handling replies from companies after manual outreach.

This document is internal only.
It does not change the app, database, or outreach automation.

---

## 1. Purpose

Use this guide when a company replies to a SmartFlow outreach email.

Main goals:

- avoid rushed answers;
- classify the reply correctly;
- update CRM status consistently;
- collect the right information;
- prepare the next step;
- avoid promising work before understanding the problem.

---

## 2. First rule

Do not answer immediately if the reply needs analysis.

Before replying:

1. Read the full message.
2. Identify the intent.
3. Open the lead in SmartFlow Lead Engine.
4. Check website, score, notes, activity history and previous outreach.
5. Update CRM status if needed.
6. Add internal notes.
7. Add a follow-up task.
8. Prepare a short, clear response.

---

## 3. Reply categories

### A. Positive / interested

Examples:

- They ask for more information.
- They agree to a call.
- They ask what SmartFlow can do.
- They ask about price or process.

CRM action:

- Move status to Replied.
- Add internal note.
- Add task: Ask for discovery call.
- Prepare discovery call response.

Next goal:

- Schedule a 15-30 minute discovery call.

---

### B. Price question

Examples:

- "How much does it cost?"
- "Can you send prices?"
- "What is your offer?"

CRM action:

- Move status to Replied.
- Add note: Price interest.
- Do not send a fixed price without basic context.

Response logic:

- Give a price range only if appropriate.
- Ask 2-3 qualifying questions.
- Suggest a short call.

Use offer ranges:

- Audit IA & Workflows: 590-900 CHF.
- Automation Quick Win: 1'200-5'000 CHF+.
- Foundation Web + IA: 3'500-7'500 CHF.
- SmartFlow Care: 149 CHF/month after delivery.

---

### C. Call request

Examples:

- They ask for a meeting.
- They propose a date.
- They ask to speak by phone.

CRM action:

- Move status to Meeting.
- Add task with meeting date.
- Add note with call purpose.

Before the call, prepare:

- website observations;
- possible quick wins;
- likely offer;
- questions to ask;
- minimum next step.

---

### D. Not interested

Examples:

- "Not interested."
- "We already have someone."
- "No need."

CRM action:

- Move status to Lost only if clearly final.
- Otherwise keep Replied and add note.
- Do not argue.

Response logic:

- Thank them.
- Keep it short.
- Leave door open.

---

### E. Wrong person

Examples:

- They say they are not responsible.
- They forward to another person.
- They provide another email.

CRM action:

- Add the correct contact if provided.
- Add note.
- Keep status Replied or move back to Analyzed if new contact must be prepared.
- Add task: Contact correct person.

---

### F. Confused or suspicious

Examples:

- They ask how SmartFlow got their contact.
- They ask why we contacted them.
- They ask what SmartFlow is.

CRM action:

- Keep status Replied.
- Add note.
- Do not become defensive.
- Answer clearly and professionally.

Response logic:

- Explain that the message was based on public business website/contact information.
- State that outreach is manual.
- Offer to stop contact if they prefer.

## 7. Discovery call questions

Ask only what is needed.

Basic questions:

1. What is the main problem with your current website or workflow?
2. Do you want more contact requests, better presentation, automation, or internal process improvement?
3. What currently takes too much time?
4. Are you already using CRM, Excel, email folders, accounting tools, or forms?
5. Do you need French only, or also English/German?
6. Is there an urgent deadline?
7. Who decides internally?
8. What would make this project successful for you?

---

## 8. After reply checklist

After any reply:

- update CRM status;
- add internal note;
- create follow-up task;
- decide next step;
- do not leave the lead without task or status.

---

## 9. Status update rules

Use:

- Replied: company answered but no meeting yet.
- Meeting: call or meeting is planned.
- Proposal: proposal is being prepared or sent.
- Won: client accepted or real agreement exists.
- Lost: not relevant, rejected, or not worth pursuing.

Do not mark Won before real agreement.
Do not mark Lost only because the reply is slow.
Do not mark Contacted unless manual outreach was actually sent.

---

## 10. Safety rules

Do not:

- promise exact results without audit;
- promise SEO ranking;
- promise guaranteed lead volume;
- invent technical problems;
- pressure the client;
- send long explanations;
- send attachments before understanding the need.

Do:

- keep answers short;
- be precise;
- ask for the next small step;
- keep CRM updated;
- keep all important context in internal notes.
