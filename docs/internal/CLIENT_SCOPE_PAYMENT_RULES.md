# SmartFlow Suisse — Client Scope and Payment Rules

## Purpose

This document defines how SmartFlow Suisse protects scope, pricing, payment, delivery expectations, and change requests before starting client work.

The goal is to avoid unclear work, unpaid implementation, oversized fixed-price promises, and uncontrolled client expectations.

---

## When to use

Use this document before:

1. sending a paid diagnostic offer;
2. sending a pilot offer;
3. starting any client implementation;
4. accepting changes after delivery;
5. preparing a devis, proposal, or scope of work;
6. deciding whether a request is included or billable.

---

## Core rule

No paid client work should start without:

- clear scope;
- clear deliverables;
- clear exclusions;
- price or billing method;
- payment terms;
- timeline;
- approval;
- risk review.

If scope is unclear, do not start implementation.

---

## 1. Offer types

SmartFlow Suisse should use controlled first offers.

### Free first discussion

Use only for initial qualification.

Includes:

- short call;
- basic understanding of the problem;
- no implementation;
- no custom workflow;
- no technical audit unless agreed.

### Paid diagnostic

Use when the client has a real problem but the solution is not clear yet.

May include:

- workflow review;
- tool review;
- lead/admin process map;
- risk review;
- first automation opportunity;
- written summary;
- recommendation for first pilot.

### First paid pilot

Use when the first workflow is clear and small enough.

May include:

- simple CRM/tracking structure;
- form or email intake workflow;
- AI-assisted internal summary;
- manual follow-up process;
- internal notification;
- QA and handover notes.

### Larger implementation

Use only after diagnostic or pilot clarity.

Requires:

- written scope;
- clear phases;
- pricing;
- timeline;
- responsibilities;
- access review;
- safety review.

---

## 2. Scope must include

Every paid offer should define:

- client name;
- project name;
- current problem;
- goal;
- deliverables;
- tools involved;
- what SmartFlow will do;
- what client must provide;
- timeline;
- price;
- payment terms;
- exclusions;
- support after delivery;
- next step.

---

## 3. Exclusions by default

Unless explicitly included, the following are excluded:

- full CRM migration;
- full website redesign;
- accounting integration;
- ERP integration;
- legal/compliance advice;
- automatic external email sending;
- mass outreach;
- complex dashboard;
- data cleanup at scale;
- emergency support;
- unlimited revisions;
- training for multiple staff members;
- new workflow after delivery;
- third-party tool subscription costs.

---

## 4. Payment terms

Default safe payment logic:

- paid diagnostic: paid before delivery or before final report;
- first paid pilot: partial payment before work starts;
- larger implementation: deposit before work starts;
- support plan: monthly recurring payment;
- change request: approved before work.

Do not rely on verbal agreement only.

Minimum internal requirement:

- written approval by email or signed quote/devis before implementation.

---

## 5. Pricing discipline

Do not underprice unclear work.

If the workflow is unclear, sell diagnostic first.

If the scope is small and clear, sell first paid pilot.

If the client wants many changes, split into phases.

Avoid large fixed-price offers when:

- tools are unknown;
- access is missing;
- data quality is unknown;
- client process is unclear;
- multiple stakeholders are involved;
- automation risk is high.

---

## 6. Change request rule

A change request is required when the client asks for:

- new workflow;
- new tool integration;
- new data source;
- new output;
- new user role;
- new dashboard;
- new website section;
- changed CRM structure;
- automatic email sending;
- different business logic;
- additional language/version;
- major redesign of what was agreed.

Before accepting:

1. capture request;
2. compare with original scope;
3. estimate effort;
4. check risk;
5. quote or approve billing;
6. implement only after approval;
7. QA after change;
8. update handover notes if needed.

---

## 7. Approval rule

Approval must be clear.

Acceptable approval:

- email confirmation;
- signed quote/devis;
- written approval in agreed channel;
- payment received where required.

Not enough:

- vague verbal approval;
- "we will see later";
- "just do it quickly";
- unclear screenshot request;
- request from non-decision maker.

---

## 8. Client responsibilities

The client may need to provide:

- access;
- correct company information;
- example data;
- current workflow explanation;
- decision maker approval;
- feedback on drafts;
- timely testing;
- payment;
- confirmation before go-live.

If the client does not provide required information, timeline can move.

---

## 9. Delivery and acceptance

Before delivery:

- QA checklist must be completed;
- handover notes must be prepared;
- limitations must be documented;
- support rules must be clear;
- next step must be defined.

Acceptance can be:

- client confirms delivery;
- client starts using the workflow;
- client approves final notes;
- agreed review period passes without blocking issue.

---

## 10. Internal decision checklist

Before starting paid work, confirm:

- problem is clear;
- first step is small enough;
- scope is written;
- price is clear;
- payment terms are clear;
- client responsibilities are clear;
- risks are understood;
- no unsafe automation is included;
- manual review remains preserved;
- there is a defined next action.

Decision:

- ready to offer;
- needs diagnostic;
- needs more information;
- too risky;
- decline.

---

## Client-facing explanation

Simple explanation:

> To keep the project safe and predictable, we define the scope, deliverables, exclusions, timeline, and payment terms before implementation. New workflows, new integrations, or larger changes are quoted separately so the system stays controlled and the budget remains clear.

---

## Final rule

No unclear scope.
No unpaid implementation.
No large fixed-price promise without review.
No change without approval.
No delivery without QA and handover.
