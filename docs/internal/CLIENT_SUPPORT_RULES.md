# SmartFlow Suisse — Client Support Rules

## Purpose

This document defines how SmartFlow Suisse handles client support after a pilot, workflow delivery, audit, CRM setup, Make scenario, AI-assisted process, website/form improvement, or first implementation.

The goal is to protect client trust, avoid unclear expectations, and prevent small projects from turning into unlimited unpaid work.

---

## When to use

Use this document:

1. before delivering a project;
2. when preparing a handover pack;
3. when offering a care plan;
4. when a client asks for changes after delivery;
5. when deciding whether a request is support, maintenance, or new scope.

---

## Core rule

Support is not unlimited implementation.

Every st-delivery request must be classified before action:

1. included support;
2. minor paid adjustment;
3. new scope;
4. urgent issue;
5. not recommended / unsafe.

---

## 1. Included support

Included support may apply only if it was agreed in the offer, pilot, care plan, or handover.

Examples:

- answer simple usage questions;
- check whether the workflow is still running;
- verify a form, sheet, or notification;
- correct a small text typo;
- explain where the result appears;
- review one small issue reported by the client;
- confirm whether a request is new scope.

Included support should stay small, practical, and time-limited.

---

## 2. Not included by default

The following are not included unless agreed separately:

- new automation;
- new Make scenario;
- new CRM structure;
- new website section;
- redesign;
- migration from another system;
- accounting integration;
- ERP integration;
- automatic external email sending;
- custom dashboard;
- complex data cleanup;
- staff training session;
- emergency weekend support;
- unlimited revisions;
- large change of workflow logic.

---

## 3. Minor paid adjustment

A minor paid adjustment may be appropriate when the request is small but outside the original scope.

Examples:

- add one field to a form and sheet;
- adjust a notification text;
- update a status list;
- change one email draft template;
- add one simple column;
- update one prompt;
- change one recipient;
- prepare one small report view.

Before doing it, confirm:

- exact change;
- estimated time;
- price or billing method;
- risk;
- whether QA is needed.

---

## 4. New scope

A request should be treated as new scope if it changes the system, workflow, logic, or business process.

Examples:

- adding a second workflow;
- connecting a new tool;
- changing CRM structure;
- adding automatic sending;
- adding multiple users or permissions;
- migrating old data;
- creating a client dashboard;
- creating a new website page;
- expanding the pilot into a full implementation.

New scope requires:

- short review;
- written scope;
- price;
- timeline;
- approval before work.

---

## 5. Urgent issue

An issue is urgent only if:

- client data is at risk;
- wrong external communication was sent or may be sent;
- production form is broken;
- business-critical workflow stopped;
- client cannot receive leads;
- access or security is compromised.

Urgent issue process:

1. pause unsafe workflow if needed;
2. collect screenshot and timestamp;
3. identify last change;
4. check source/input;
5. check Make/scenario logs if applicable;
6. check output destination;
7. apply the smallest safe fix;
8. document what happened.

---

## 6. Unsafe requests

Some requests should not be accepted without extra review.

Examples:

- automatic external email sending;
- mass outreach;
- scraping personal data;
- storing passwords in plain documents;
- bypassing client approval;
- changing DNS without backup;
- editing production without testing;
- processing sensitive data without clear need;
- promising legal, tax, or compliance guarantees.

Response:

Pause, review risk, and explain safer alternatives.

---

## 7. Response time

Default internal guidance:

- simple question: reply when available;
- minor issue: review within agreed support window;
- urgent issue: prioritize if covered by agreement;
- new scope: estimate separately;
- unclear request: ask for clarification before acting.

Do not promise 24/7 support unless it is explicitly sold and priced.

---

## 8. Monthly care plan logic

Possible care plan levels:

### Basic care

For simple website/form/workflow clients.

May include:

- monthly check;
- form test;
- notification check;
- small text edits;
- light support.

### Automation care

For Make/CRM/AI workflow clients.

May include:

- scenario monitoring;
- small workflow adjustments;
- monthly review;
- prompt tuning;
- basic reporting.

### Advanced operations support

For larger clients.

May include:

- multiple workflows;
- team support;
- dashboard review;
- quarterly improvement plan;
- priority handling.

Do not offer advanced support before understanding workload.

---

## 9. Change request process

For any non-trivial change:

1. capture request;
2. classify it;
3. check current scope;
4. estimate effort;
5. identify risks;
6. confirm price or approval;
7. implement safely;
8. QA check;
9. update handover notes if needed.

---

## 10. Internal classification

For every support request, record:

- client:
- date:
- request:
- category:
- included / paid / new scope / urgent / unsafe:
- risk:
- estimated effort:
- decision:
- next action:

---

## Client-facing explanation

Simple explanation:

> After delivery, small agreed checks and minor support are handled according to the support plan. New workflows, new integrations, structural changes, or larger improvements are quoted separately so the scope stays clear and the system remains safe.

---

## Final rule

Protect the client, protect SmartFlow, and keep every change controlled.

No unclear work.
No unlimited support.
No unsafe automation.
No change without scope.
