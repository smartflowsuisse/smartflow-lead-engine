# 4. Proposal Structure

Use after a discovery call or clear audit.

## Proposal sections

1. Context
2. Observed problem
3. Recommended solution
4. Deliverables
5. Timeline
6. Price
7. Client responsibilities
8. What is not included
9. Next step

## Context

Shortly explain why SmartFlow is proposing this.

Example:

The current website already provides a base for online visibility, but the contact path and conversion structure can be improved to generate clearer incoming requests.

## Observed problem

List only 2–4 important points.

Do not overload the client with technical details.

## Recommended solution

Choose one main offer:

- Audit IA & Workflows
- Automation Quick Win
- Foundation Web + IA

Optional add-on:

- SmartFlow Care

## Deliverables

Write exactly what the client receives.

## Timeline

Use realistic ranges:

- Audit: 3–7 days
- Quick Win: 3–10 days
- Foundation Web + IA: 7–21 days

## Price

Use clear indicative price.

## Client responsibilities

List what client must provide:

- confirmation;
- logo;
- photos;
- text;
- access if required;
- feedback on preview.

## Not included

Clarify limits:

- full rebrand;
- advanced custom software;
- paid ads;
- large integrations;
- legal copywriting;
- hosting costs unless specified.

## Next step

Simple CTA:

- approve proposal;
- schedule call;
- send required materials;
- confirm start date.

# 7. SmartFlow Care Follow-up

Use after project delivery.

## When to offer

Offer after successful delivery, not before.

## Positioning

SmartFlow Care keeps the website and contact flow monitored after launch.

## Includes

- form checks;
- email notification checks;
- small text/photo updates;
- monthly review;
- basic support.

## Does not include

- redesign;
- new website;
- complex integrations;
- major new functionality;
- paid ads management.

## Suggested message

After the project is delivered, we can also keep the website monitored through SmartFlow Care. This includes basic checks, small updates and monthly support, so the website remains reliable after launch.

# 8. Internal Status Rules

## Lead statuses

New:
Lead exists but has not been analyzed.

Analyzed:
Lead has been reviewed or analyzed.

Contacted:
Manual email was actually sent.

Replied:
Company replied.

Meeting:
Call is planned or completed.

Proposal:
Proposal sent.

Won:
Client accepted.

Lost:
Client declined or no fit.

## Important rule

Never mark a lead as Contacted before a real manual email has been sent.
