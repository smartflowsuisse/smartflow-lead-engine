# SmartFlow First Client Manual Scenario

## Purpose

This document defines one practical manual scenario for handling a first real client opportunity safely.

The goal is to move from client reply to mini-audit, limited next-step proposal, and manual follow-up without improvisation, automatic sending, mass outreach, or production risk.

This is an internal operating scenario, not a client-facing document.

## Scenario overview

1. Receive a real client reply.
2. Review the reply manually.
3. Verify the client context.
4. Prepare internal notes.
5. Prepare a short mini-audit or one-page summary if useful.
6. Prepare a limited mini-proposal only if enough context exists.
7. Prepare a Gmail draft.
8. Review the draft manually.
9. Send manually only after approval.
10. Update Gmail label and CRM / Lead Engine status manually.
11. Create a follow-up reminder if needed.

## Step 1 — Classify the reply

Classify the reply before answering:

- Interested: the client asks for details, accepts a call, or shares information.
- Cautious: the client asks questions or wants clarification.
- Not ready: the client asks to follow up later.
- Not relevant: the client declines or the fit is poor.
- Sensitive: the client mentions confidential data, access, credentials, finance, legal, or private client information.

Do not use automatic reply sending.

## Step 2 — Apply Gmail label

Use SmartFlow Gmail manually.

Apply the most relevant label:

- `02 Client Replies` for new replies;
- `03 Proposals` when a proposal is being prepared or has been sent;
- `04 Follow-up` when a future reminder is needed;
- `05 Make Demo` when the reply is related to demo workflow;
- `06 Technical / Accounts` for account, access, Make, Drive, or Gmail issues.

## Step 3 — Verify client context

Before preparing any client-facing text, verify:

- company name;
- contact person;
- email address;
- website;
- language;
- industry;
- source of conversation;
- what the client actually asked;
- what is visible and confirmed;
- what is still unknown.

Do not invent facts.

If there is not enough information, prepare a short clarification reply instead of a proposal.

## Step 4 — Prepare internal notes

Use this structure:

Company:
Contact:
Language:
Website:
Source:
Reply category:
Observed need:
Known facts:
Unknowns:
Risk level:
Recommended next step:

Separate visible facts from assumptions.

## Step 5 — Choose the correct material

Open:

- `FIRST_CLIENT_PACK_DRAFT.md` for the operating pack;
- `FIRST_CLIENT_REPLY_HANDLING.md` for reply handling;
- `FRENCH_ONE_PAGE_CLIENT_SUMMARY_TEMPLATE.md` for a short French mini-audit style summary;
- `FIRST_CLIENT_MINI_PROPOSAL_STRUCTURE.md` for a limited next-step proposal;
- `FRENCH_DISCOVERY_EMAIL_TEMPLATES.md` only for manually adapted French wording.

Do not copy a template blindly.

## Step 6 — Prepare mini-audit notes

Prepare a short mini-audit only from visible or confirmed information.

Use:

- website observation;
- form or intake observation;
- follow-up process observation;
- CRM or tracking gap if visible;
- client reply details;
- demo data only when needed.

Do not claim hidden problems.

Do not promise fixed savings.

## Step 7 — Prepare one-page client summary

If useful, draft a short summary using:

- `FRENCH_ONE_PAGE_CLIENT_SUMMARY_TEMPLATE.md`

The summary should explain:

- what was ob may be unclear or inefficient;
- what can be improved;
- what SmartFlow can safely propose;
- what the next step should be.

The summary must remain short and cautious.

## Step 8 — Prepare limited mini-proposal

Prepare a mini-proposal only if there is enough context.

Use:

- `FIRST_CLIENT_MINI_PROPOSAL_STRUCTURE.md`

The proposal should be:

- short;
- specific;
- based on the real client workflow;
- limited in scope;
- safe to execute;
- manually reviewed before sending.

Do not propose a large project before the workflow is understood.

## Step 9 — Prepare Gmail draft

Use Gmail draft templates only as a starting point:

- `TEMPLATE — New lead reply FR`;
- `TEMPLATE — Follow-up FR`;
- `TEMPLATE — Mini-audit delivery FR`;
- `TEMPLATE — Proposal delivery FR`.

Before sending, manually check:

- recipient email;
- company name;
- contact name;
- language;
- subject line;
- all placeholders removed;
- no unsupported claims;
- no fixed savings;
- no sensitive data;
- no automatic sending;
- no mass outreach wording.

## Step 10 — Manual approval before sending

Before any external message, confirm:

- the message is manually reviewed;
- French wording is manually reviewed;
- the next step is clear;
- the message is short;
- the tone is professional;
- the offer is limited and safe;
- no production work is promised;
- no Make schedule is enabled;
- no automatic email sending is used.

If any point is unclear, do not send.

## Step 11 — Manual sending only

If approved, send manually from Gmail.

After sending:

- apply the correct Gmail label;
- update CRM / Lead Engine status manually if needed;
- mark as Contacted only if real manual sending happened;
- create follow-up reminder if needed;
- record the next action.

## Safety rules

- No automatic external email sending.
- No mass outreach.
- No Make schedule enabled without review.
- No production changes without tests and QA.
- No sensitive data exposure.
- No fixed savings without real analysis.
- No client-facing message without manual review.
- Gmail drafts are templates only.
- Every client-facing message must be adapted to the real client.
- Use demo or low-risk data whenever possible.
