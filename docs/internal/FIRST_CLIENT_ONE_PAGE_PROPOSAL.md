# First Client One-Page Proposal

## Purpose

This document defines a short first-client proposal for SmartFlow Suisse.

It is designed to be used after a first demo or discovery conversation.

The proposal should stay simple, practical, and focused on one controlled pilot workflow.

---

## Proposal Title

SmartFlow Lead & Follow-up Pilot

A controlled workflow to structure incoming requests, prepare follow-up, and preview client conversion before human approval.

---

## 1. Client Problem

Many small and medium businesses receive requests through different channels such as website forms, email, phone notes, spreadsheets, or internal messages.

This often creates several problems:

- requests are hard to track;
- follow-up depends on manual memory;
- urgent leads can be missed;
- information is scattered across tools;
- response time becomes slower;
- business owners do not always know what should happen next.

The result is lost time, missed opportunities, and unnecessary administrative work.

---

## 2. Proposed Solution

SmartFlow Suisse proposes a controlled Lead & Follow-up pilot.

The goal is to organize one request process from first contact to follow-up preparation and conversion preview.

The system helps the team:

- capture and structure incoming requests;
- summarize the request;
- prepare the next action;
- review follow-up manually;
- preview what would happen before creating a client;
- keep human approval before any external or irreversible action.

Core principle:

The workflow prepares the work. The human approves the action.

---

## 3. Demo / Pilot Workflow

The proposed workflow is:

Request
→ Lead Engine
→ Client Hub
→ AI-assisted summary
→ Manual follow-up preparation
→ Convert to Client preview
→ Human approval

The pilot is controlled and does not perform automatic external sending or real client creation without approval.

---

## 4. What SmartFlow Will Implement

The first pilot can include:

- review of one current request process;
- definition of required lead fields;
- structured lead workflow;
- Client Hub demo view;
- AI-assisted summary and next-action support;
- manual follow-up preparation;
- convert-to-client preview;
- approval and safety rules;
- QA checklist before demo or pilot use;
- short handover documentation.

The first scope should focus on one clear workflow, not the full business.

---

## 5. What The Client Provides

To start the pilot, the client should provide:

- one priority request process;
- examples of typical requests;
- current follow-up method;
- preferred communication language;
- current tools used by the team;
- one responsible contact person;
- approval for a controlled pilot scope.

No large internal transformation is required for the first pilot.

---

## 6. Timeline

Estimated timeline:

7 to 21 days

Typical phases:

1. Discovery and process mapping.
2. Demo workflow setup.
3. Review with realistic examples.
4. Adjustments and handover.

The timeline depends on process complexity, available examples, and client responsiveness.

---

## 7. Price Range

Recommended first pilot range:

CHF 1,500 to CHF 4,500

The final price depends on:

- number of intake channels;
- workflow complexity;
- required languages;
- existing tools;
- customization level;
- documentation needs;
- whether website or form changes are included.

A simple first workflow can start at the lower end.

A more complete workflow with Client Hub demo, AI summary, follow-up preparation, and approval policy should be positioned closer to the middle or upper part of the range.

---

## 8. Safety Policy

The pilot follows a human-approval-first policy.

During the pilot:

- no email is sent automatically;
- no client is created automatically;
- no lead status is changed without review;
- no production automation is enabled without explicit approval;
- no mass outreach is included;
- no irreversible action is performed without validation.

## 9. What Is Not Included

The first pilot does not include:

- full CRM replacement;
- complex permissions;
- accounting integration;
- autonomous AI agent actions;
- automatic email campaigns;
- mass outreach;
- WhatsApp or Telegram automation;
- production database migration;
- long-term maintenance unless agreed separately.

Additional features can be planned after the first pilot is validated.

---

## 10. Recommended Next Step

The recommended next step is a short discovery call.

The goal of the call is to define one pilot workflow:

1. Which request process should be improved first?
2. Where do requests arrive today?
3. Who handles follow-up?
4. What information is required before replying?
5. What should never happen automatically?
6. What would make the pilot successful?

After this call, SmartFlow Suisse can prepare a specific implementation proposal.
