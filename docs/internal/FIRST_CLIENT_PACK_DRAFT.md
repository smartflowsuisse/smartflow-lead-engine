# SmartFlow First Client Pack Draft

## Purpose

This document defines the minimum working pack to prepare for a first real client conversation, reply, mini-audit, or manual follow-up.

The goal is to avoid improvisation and use one clear, safe, reviewed workflow.

This is an internal operating document, not a client-facing document.

## When to use this pack

Use this pack when:

- a real prospect replies;
- a discovery call is planned;
- a mini-audit needs to be prepared;
- a short client summary is needed;
- a limited next-step proposal is needed.

Do not use this pack for:

- mass outreach;
- automatic email sending;
- production changes;
- unreviewed client-facing messages;
- promises of fixed savings or guaranteed results.

## First-client workflow

### Step 1 — Identify the client context

Check manually:

- company name;
- website;
- language;
- contact person;
- email address;
- industry;
- source of request;
- visible business pain;
- whether the lead is real or only demo/test data.

### Step 2 — Prepare internal notes

Write short internal notes before any client-facing text:

- what the company does;
- what problem seems visible;
- what process may be improved;
- what information is missing;
- what next question should be asked.

Do not assume facts that are not visible or confirmed.

### Step 3 — Choose the right client-facing material

Use:

- reply handling document if the client has replied;
- one-page summary template if a mini-audit style summary is needed;
- mini-proposal structure only after enough context is known;
- discovery email templates only for manually reviewed French wording.

### Step 4 — Prepare a draft only

Prepare a draft in Gmail or locally.

Do not send automatically.

Before sending, verify:

- recipient email;
- company name;
- client language;
- no sensitive data exposure;
- no fixed savings promise;
- no unsupported claim;
- no mass outreach;
- no automation triggered;
- no Make schedule enabled.

### Step 5 — Human review

Before any external message:

- review the French wording manually;
- adapt placeholders;
- remove generic wording;
- confirm the message fits the real business;
- confirm the message is short and clear;
- confirm SmartFlow is proposing only a safe next step.

### Step 6 — Manual sending only

If approved, send manually from Gmail.

After manual sending:

- apply the correct Gmail label;
- update CRM / Lead Engine status manually if needed;
- create follow-up reminder if needed;
- do not mark as contacted unless real manual sending happened.

## Safe first-client next steps

Preferred next steps:

- propose a short 20–30 minute discovery call;
- propose a small mini-audit;
- propose a limited demo using demo data;
- propose a low-risk first workflow review.

Avoid:

- large project proposal too early;
- technical implementation before understanding workflow;
- production changes before agreement and QA;
- automatic sending;
- broad AI promises;
- guaranteed savings.

## Safety rules

- No automatic external email sending.
- No mass outreach.
- No Make schedule enabled without review.
- No production changes without tests and QA.
- No sensitive data exposure.
- No fixed savings without real analysis.
- No client-facing message without manual review.
- Gmail drafts are templates only.
- Every client-facing message must be adapted to the real client.
- Use demo or low-risk data whenever possible.
