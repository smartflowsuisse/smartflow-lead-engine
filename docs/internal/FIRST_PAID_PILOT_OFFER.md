# SmartFlow First Paid Pilot Offer

## Purpose

This document defines the first simple paid pilot offer for SmartFlow Suisse.

The goal is not to sell a large project immediately.

The goal is to convert the first serious conversation into a small paid pilot that proves value, creates trust, and can become a larger implementation later.

This is an internal operating document. Client-facing wording must be adapted manually before use.

## Target client

Best first target:

- Swiss small or medium business;
- Suisse romande first;
- construction, menuiserie, renovation, real estate services, home services, or local professional services;
- receives client requests by email, phone, website form, referral, or WhatsApp;
- does not have a clear follow-up process;
- risks losing requests, forgetting follow-ups, or replying too late.

## Core business problem

Many small businesses do not lose clients because they lack demand.

They lose opportunities because requests are scattered:

- emails;
- phone calls;
- website forms;
- manual notes;
- repeated follow-ups;
- no clear CRM;
- no next action owner;
- no visibility on what has been answered and what is still pending.

## Pilot name

SmartFlow Lead & Follow-up Pilot

## Simple client-facing promise

SmartFlow helps your team centralize incoming requests, understand them faster, and prepare the next manual action without losing control.

No automatic external email is sent.

The human keeps control.

## What the pilot includes

### 1. Current workflow review

Review how requests currently arrive and are followed:

- website form;
- email;
- phone;
- manual notes;
- spreadsheet;
- CRM if already used.

### 2. Simple CRM structure

Prepare or improve a lightweight CRM view with fields such as:

- company / client name;
- contact;
- request type;
- message;
- source;
- priority;
- status;
- next action;
- follow-up date;
- internal notes.

### 3. AI-assisted internal summary

Prepare a safe AI-assisted summary for each request:

- client need;
- urgency;
- priority;
- possible next action;
- internal note for the team.

This is internal only.

### 4. Manual follow-up workflow

Define a simple manual follow-up process:

- who reviews the request;
- who prepares the reply;
- when to follow up;
- when to mark contacted;
- when to create a task;
- when to stop.

### 5. First demo or pilot workflow

Demonstrate a controlled workflow:

Google Form or request source → Google Sheet / CRM → Make manual run → AI internal summary → CRM row → manual review.

### 6. Handover notes

Provide short internal notes explaining:

- how the pilot works;
- what the client team should check;
- what should not be automated yet;
- what the next improvement could be.

## What the pilot does not include

The first pilot does not include:

- full CRM migration;
- complex database implementation;
- automatic external email sending;
- mass outreach;
- production system changes without review;
- replacing the client’s full operations process;
- custom public SaaS build;
- long-term maintenance unless separately agreed.

## Questions for the first client call

Ask:

1. How do new requests arrive today?
2. Who checks them?
3. Where are they stored?
4. How do you know who replied?
5. How do you know when to follow up?
6. What is usually forgotten or delayed?
7. What takes too much manual time?
8. Do you already use a CRM or spreadsheet?
9. Would a simple request-tracking workflow help?
10. What would be a useful first improvement in 3 to 5 days?
