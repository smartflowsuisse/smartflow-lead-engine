# SmartFlow One-Page Client Audit Structure

## Purpose

This document defines a simple one-page audit structure for a first SmartFlow client.

The goal is to understand the client's current workflow before proposing automation.

This is an internal draft.

## Audit principle

The audit should be:

- short;
- practical;
- specific;
- focused on one workflow;
- safe for the client;
- based on observation, not assumptions.

Do not use the audit to sell a large project too early.

## When to use this audit

Use it after:

- a positive reply;
- a short discovery call;
- a first demo;
- a website review that shows a clear workflow opportunity.

Do not use it for mass outreach.

## Audit output

The audit should produce one clear recommendation:

- workflow audit;
- small automation quick win;
- CRM/contact-form cleanup;
- follow-up process improvement;
- website/contact conversion improvement;
- no action if the fit is weak.

## One-page audit sections

### 1. Company context

Record:

- company name;
- industry;
- location;
- website;
- language;
- main contact;
- source of lead;
- date reviewed.

### 2. Current client intake

Check how new requests arrive:

- website form;
- email;
- phone;
- WhatsApp or SMS;
- referral;
- direct visit;
- manual notes;
- spreadsheet;
- existing CRM.

Questions:

- Where do requests arrive first?
- Who sees them?
- Who answers them?
- Where are they tracked?
- What happens if nobody follows up?

### 3. Follow-up process

Check:

- whether there is a clear next action;
- whether follow-ups are assigned;
- whether reminders exist;
- whether old requests are forgotten;
- whether quote requests are tracked.

Questions:

- How do you know which request needs attention today?
- How are follow-ups planned?
- What is usually delayed?
- What is repeated every week?

### 4. Website and contact friction

Check:

- contact form visibility;
- phone visibility;
- email visibility;
- mobile usability;
- clarity of service pages;
- speed of finding contact information;
- whether the next step is obvious.

Do not over-focus on design polish.

Focus on whether the website helps generate and track real requests.

### 5. Data and safety

Check:

- what client data is collected;
- where it is stored;
- who has access;
- whether sensitive data appears;
- what should not be sent to AI;
- what must stay private.

Default rule:

Use fake or low-risk demo data unless real data is necessary and explicitly approved.

### 6. Automation opportunity

Look for one small workflow improvement.

Examples:

- form submission to CRM row;
- automatic internal notification;
- AI summary for internal review;
- draft reply without sending;
- follow-up task creation;
- lead priority label;
- missing contact data checklist.

Avoid:

- automatic external sending;
- mass outreach;
- replacing human decisions;
- connecting too many systems at once;
- changing production during demo.

### 7. Recommended quick win

Choose one.

Good quick wins:

- clean contact form flow;
- simple CRM sheet;
- internal notification;
- AI summary draft;
- follow-up reminder;
- reply template;
- status pipeline;
- lead intake cleanup.

Bad first quick wins:

- full CRM migration;
- complex multi-system automation;
- automatic email sending;
- broad AI assistant without workflow boundaries;
- public SaaS setup.

### 8. Risk assessment

Record risks:

- data sensitivity;
- unclear ownership;
- unverified email addresses;
- missing approval process;
- weak internal adoption;
- over-automation;
- production impact.

Use simple risk labels:

- Low;
- Medium;
- High.

If risk is high, do not automate first.

Recommend discovery or cleanup first.

### 9. Proposed next step

Offer one clear next step:

- paid workflow audit;
- small automation quick win;
- CRM/contact-form cleanup;
- follow-up proposal;
- no action for now.

Do not offer multiple confusing options at once.

## Internal scoring

Optional simple score:

### Contact readiness

- 0 = no clear contact channel;
- 1 = phone or email exists;
- 2 = contact form exists;
- 3 = clear contact form + email/phone + mobile friendly.

### Workflow clarity

- 0 = unknown;
- 1 = manual and unclear;
- 2 = partly structured;
- 3 = clear process with visible next steps.

### Automation fit

- 0 = no obvious fit;
- 1 = possible but unclear;
- 2 = clear small workflow;
- 3 = strong quick-win opportunity.

### Safety readiness

- 0 = sensitive or unclear data risk;
- 1 = some risk;
- 2 = manageable with limits;
- 3 = safe with demo data and human approval.

## Recommendation logic

If contact readiness is low:

- recommend contact-form or website intake cleanup.

If workflow clarity is low:

- recommend discovery call or workflow audit.

If automation fit is high and safety readiness is high:

- recommend small automation quick win.

If safety readiness is low:

- do not automate yet;
- clarify data and approval rules first.

## Safety rules

- No automatic external email sending.
- No mass outreach.
- Human approval required.
- No sensitive data exposure.
- No production changes during audit.
- No overpromising.
- No fixed savings without measurement.
- Do not use real client data unless necessary and approved.
