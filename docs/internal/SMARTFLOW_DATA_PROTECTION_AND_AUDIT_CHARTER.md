# SmartFlow Suisse — Data Protection & Audit Charter

## Purpose

This document defines the internal SmartFlow Suisse rules for audits, client preparation, AI workflows, automation, access handling, and data protection.

SmartFlow Suisse works with a data-protection-first approach under Swiss nDSG/FADP principles.

This document is internal and operational. It is not a public legal contract.

---

## 1. Core rule

SmartFlow Suisse follows this principle:

**Safety, transparency, and data minimization come before automation.**

This means:

- do not collect unnecessary data;
- do not store data without a clear purpose;
- do not use real client data where demo/fake data is enough;
- do not access closed systems without clie permission;
- do not enable automatic external email sending without separate approval;
- do not run mass scraping or mass outreach;
- do not put client credentials in chats, notes, Google Docs, or spreadsheets.

---

## 2. Before signed or approved client work

Before a client has approved the work, SmartFlow Suisse may analyze only public company website elements.

Allowed:

- public website pages;
- visible forms;
- UX and structure;
- mobile experience;
- SEO basics;
- lead flow;
- public offers and CTAs;
- public business process signals.

Not allowed without separate permission:

- CRM access;
- email inbox access;
- Google Analytics access;
- Search Console access;
- Google Drive access;
- Make/Zapier/client automation access;
- internal client files;
- customer data;
- private documents;
- closed areas of websites;
- real lead data;
- passwords or API keys.

---

## 3. Public company data vs personal data

Public company information can usually be reviewed for an audit.

Examples:

- company name;
- public website;
- public services;
- public pages;
- visible offers;
- visible contact forms;
- public lead journey.

Personal data must be handled carefully.

Examples of personal data:

- names of employees;
- personal email addresses;
- direct phone numbers;
- LinkedIn profiles;
- contact form submissions;
- CRM records;
- email conversations;
- customer data;
- analytics data linked to identifiable users;
- call recordings;
- documents containing identifiable people.

SmartFlow Suisse must avoid collecting, copying, enriching, or storing personal data unless there is a clear purpose and permission.

---

## 4. Data minimization rule

SmartFlow Suisse stores only the minimum data needed for the task.

Before saving any data, check:

1. Why do we need this data?
2. Can we complete the task without it?
3. Is there a less risky option?
4. Where will it be stored?
5. Who has access?
6. When will it be deleted or archived?

Data must never be stored “just in case”.

---

## 5. Demo and test data rule

For tests, demos, Make scenarios, Google Sheets, CRM demos, and AI workflows, SmartFlow Suisse uses demo/fake data.

Allowed examples:

- Demo Construction Sàrl;
- Demo Menuiserie Genève;
- Claire Demo;
- Jean Test;
- fake lead;
- fake phone;
- fake email;
- fake company;
- fake CRM row.

Real personal data must not be used in tests unless the client has approved it and the use is necessary.

---

## 6. Client permission rule

Before real client work, SmartFlow Suisse must clearly confirm what will be reviewed.

For website/audit work, client intake should include:

“I confirm that SmartFlow Suisse may review our public website, visible forms, public pages and related lead flow for the purpose of preparing an audit, proposal or automation recommendation. No internal systems, private accounts, customer data, CRM, email inbox, analytics or confidential documents will be accessed without separate explicit permission.”

French version:

“Je confirme que SmartFlow Suisse peut analyser notre site web public, les formulaires visibles, les pages publiques et le parcours de génération de leads afin de préparer un audit, une proposition ou une recommandation d’automatisation. Aucun système interne, compte privé, donnée client, CRM, boîte email, outil analytics ou document confidentiel ne sera consulté sans autorisation explicite séparée.”

---

## 7. Access and credentials

Client credentials must only be stored in a password manager.

Allowed:

- 1Password;
- Bitwarden;
- another secure password manager.

Not allowed:

- ChatGPT chat;
- normal notes;
- Google Docs;
- Google Sheets;
- screenshots;
- plain text files;
- email threads without protection.

Each client should have a separate protected section or vault when possible.

---

## 8. Google Drive and client files

Client files must not be mixed with personal accounts.

Recommended folder structure:

CLIENT_NAME/
- 00_Admin
- 01_Discovery
- 02_Intake
- 03_Audit
- 04_Proposal_Devis
- 05_Implementation
- 06_Access_Notes
- 07_QA_Handover
- 08_Care_Plan

Access should be given only when needed.

---

## 9. AI tools

AI tools may be used for:

- audit notes;
- public website analysis;
- proposal drafts;
- checklists;
- summaries;
- demo workflows;
- fake/demo data processing.

AI tools must not be used carelessly for:

- real personal data;
- customer databases;
- CRM exports;
- confidential documents;
- email inboxes;
- lead data;
- medical, financial, legal, or sensitive data.

Rule:

**If fake/demo data is enough, use fake/demo data.**

---

## 10. Outreach and email

SmartFlow Suisse does not run mass outreach.

Allowed:

- manual targeted outreach;
- individual company review;
- 10–20 carefully selected contacts per week;
- manual draft review;
- manual sending after human approval.

Not allowed:

- mass email sending;
- automatic cold outreach;
- scraping email databases;
- automatic external email sending from Make;
- sending without human review.

Rule:

**Every external email must be reviewed by a human before sending.**

---

## 11. Make and automation safety

Allowed safe workflows:

- Google Form to Google Sheet;
- Google Sheet to AI summary;
- AI summary to CRM row;
- internal notification;
- Gmail draft without sending;
- manual review step.

Not allowed without separate approval:

- automatic external email sending;
- mass outreach;
- automatic modification of client data;
- automatic processing of real client leads without review;
- access to client systems without permission.

Rule:

**Manual review first. Automation second.**

---

## 12. Audit checklist rule

Every audit must include a data protection check:

- only public data reviewed;
- no unnecessary personal data copied;
- no mass email collection;
- no closed system access;
- no real personal data in tests;
- client permission checked;
- access storage checked;
- unnecessary data deleted after use;
- password manager used for credentials.

---

## 13. Proposal and devis rule

Every proposal/devis should define:

- what will be analyzed;
- which systems are involved;
- which data is needed;
- which data is not needed;
- who provides access;
- where materials are stored;
- what is excluded;
- that email automation is not enabled without approval.

---

## 14. Handover rule

At the end of a project, the client should receive a handover pack with:

- what was done;
- what files were created;
- what automations were configured;
- what access was used;
- what data was processed;
- remaining risks;
- what the client must monitor;
- what is included in Care Plan;
- what is not included in Care Plan.

---

## 15. What SmartFlow Suisse does not do now

Current exclusions:

- no heavy CRM;
- no public SaaS;
- no mass outreach;
- no automatic cold email;
- no complex accounting system;
- no client password storage in documents;
- no sensitive data processing without separate review;
- no automatic external email sending from Make;
- no access to closed systems without permission.

---

## 16. Minimum operating kit before first client

Before taking a real client into implementation, SmartFlow Suisse should have:

1. Discovery Call Template.
2. Client Intake Form.
3. Audit Checklist.
4. Mini Audit Template.
5. Proposal Template.
6. Devis / Invoice process.
7. Client Folder Template.
8. Password Manager structure.
9. QA Checklist.
10. Handover Pack.
11. Care Plan offer.
12. Mock-client test.

---

## 17. Final rule

SmartFlow Suisse must not do fast work that creates risk for the client, data, reputation, or access.

Safe sequence:

1. Check current status.
2. Identify which data is involved.
3. Minimize data.
4. Get permission if needed.
5. Use demo/fake data for tests.
6. Verify result.
7. Implement only after review.
