# Controlled Paid Pilot Outreach Process

Document type: Controlled outreach process
Status: Draft
Audience: Internal use before any next company is contacted
Related roadmap step: Step 6 — Controlled Outreach Process

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how to contact each next company safely for the SmartFlow Lead & Follow-up paid pilot.

The goal is to keep outreach controlled, manual, limited, and review-based.

This is not a mass outreach process.

This is not an automated email process.

This document does not require any Make, Google Sheets, Gmail, production, Vercel, API, or database change.

---

## 2. Core Rule

Only one company should be prepared and contacted at a time.

Do not contact the next company until the previous company has:

- public mini-audit prepared;
- manual email draft prepared;
- manual review completed;
- manual send approved;
- CRM update rule clear;
- follow-up task prepared.

---

## 3. Required Process

For each company:

1. Select one company.
2. Prepare public mini-audit.
3. Prepare manual email draft.
4. Review manually.
5. Send manually only after approval.
6. Mark Contacted only after confirmed send.
7. Create follow-up task.
8. Add activity history.

No step should be skipped.

---

## 4. Company Selection Rules

A company can be selected only if:

- there is a clear public contact path;
- the company is relevant to the Lead & Follow-up Pilot;
- the public mini-audit can be prepared without private data;
- no unsupported claims are required;
- there is no reason to delay due to unclear contact data;
- sending one manual email would be appropriate.

Do not select a company if:

- the contact path is unclear;
- only private or scraped personal data is available;
- the company requires separate verification first;
- the outreach would look like mass emailing;
- there is no relevant pilot angle.

---

## 5. Public Mini-Audit Rules

Before any email draft is prepared, create a public-only mini-audit.

The mini-audit must use only:

- public website pages;
- public contact path;
- public service positioning;
- public company information visible without private access.

The mini-audit must not include:

- assumptions about internal CRM;
- assumptions about internal sales process;
- assumptions about internal team workflow;
- claims about lost leads;
- claims about slow response;
- criticism that cannot be supported by public observation.

Safe framing:

- public contact path reviewed;
- potential improvement opportunity;
- manual-first follow-up support;
- no automatic external replies;
- human review remains mandatory.

---

## 6. Manual Email Draft Rules

After the public mini-audit is prepared, create a manual email draft.

The draft must:

- be short;
- be polite;
- be in the appropriate language;
- mention a concrete public observation;
- avoid pressure;
- avoid guaranteed outcomes;
- avoid claiming the company has a broken process;
- offer a small next step;
- remain manual.

The draft must not:

- be sent automatically;
- be part of a sequence;
- be sent to multiple companies at once;
- include unsupported claims;
- promise automatic email sending;
- promise guaranteed leads or revenue;
- suggest replacing the company CRM or team.

---

## 7. Manual Review Before Sending

Before sending any email, manually confirm:

- company name is correct;
- recipient email is correct;
- language is correct;
- public observation is accurate;
- no unsupported internal claim is included;
- no guaranteed outcome is promised;
- no automatic sending is promised;
- offer is aligned with the one-page pilot;
- reply handling document is ready;
- follow-up task is ready;
- CRM update after send is clear.

If any item is unclear, do not send.

---

## 8. Manual Send Rule

Emails may be sent only manually after review.

Allowed:

- one reviewed manual email to one selected company;
- manual send from the approved SmartFlow email account;
- CRM update only after confirmed send.

Not allowed:

- automatic sending;
- Gmail module in Make;
- Make email module;
- mass outreach;
- scheduled sequence;
- unreviewed send;
- send before public mini-audit exists;
- send before follow-up logic exists.

---

## 9. CRM Update Rule

After confirmed manual send:

- mark the company according to the CRM status rules;
- record the sent date;
- record the language;
- record the subject;
- record the recipient;
- record the outreach type;
- add a short activity note;
- create a follow-up task.

Do not mark as Contacted before confirmed send.

Do not mark as Replied before a real reply exists.

Do not mark as Paid Pilot before explicit agreement exists.

---

## 10. Follow-Up Rule

If no reply is received:

- wait at least 5-7 business days;
- send at most one polite manual follow-up;
- review the follow-up before sending;
- log the follow-up after sending;
- do not continue pushing after no response.

If the company says no:

- reply politely if needed;
- do not push;
- do not follow up again unless they reopen the conversation.

If the company is interested:

- use the reply handling document;
- keep the next reply short;
- offer mini-audit or call only when useful;
- log the reply and next action.

---

## 11. Stop Conditions

Stop the outreach process if:

- contact data is unclear;
- public evidence is insufficient;
- email draft requires unsupported claims;
- the company is not relevant to the pilot;
- the process begins to look like mass outreach;
- there is pressure to send before review;
- automation is suggested before manual validation;
- CRM update rules are unclear.

When a stop condition occurs, pause and review before continuing.

---

## 12. Explicitly Not Allowed

Do not:

- recheck Make or Sheets without a specific reason;
- enable Make schedule;
- add a Gmail module to Make;
- create automatic email sending;
- start mass outreach;
- change production;
- deploy to Vercel;
- change database schema;
- start public SaaS work;
- polish UI unless it directly supports first-client execution;
- create new features outside this roadmap.

---

## 13. Next Candidate Order

The next company must be selected only after the current roadmap step requires it.

Potential candidates mentioned in the roadmap should stay blocked until the required previous steps are complete.

Do not contact any next company directly from this document.
