# Paid Pilot Demo Call Checklist

Document type: Paid pilot demo call checklist
Status: Draft
Audience: Internal use before and during a prospect demo call
Related roadmap step: Step 5 — Paid Pilot Demo Call Checklist

---

## 1. Purpose

This document prepares a short 15-minute call flow for presenting the SmartFlow Lead & Follow-up Pilot.

The goal is to keep the call clear, practical, safe, and focused on a small paid pilot.

This is not a sales script for mass outreach.

This checklist does not require any production, Vercel, API, database, Make, Google Sheets, Gmail, or email automation change.

---

## 2. Call Objective

The call has three objectives:

1. Understand how the prospect currently receives and follows incoming requests.
2. Show a simple manual-first demo concept.
3. Confirm whether a small paid pilot is worth preparing.

The call should not try to close a large project.

The call should not promise full automation.

The call should not show or promise automatic external email sending.

---

## 3. Before the Call

Before the call, confirm:

- [ ] Demo before client QA checklist is reviewed.
- [ ] Browser tabs are prepared.
- [ ] Only demo-safe tabs are open.
- [ ] No private inbox is visible.
- [ ] No sensitive client data is visible.
- [ ] Make demo remains manual.
- [ ] Run once only is used if a demo run is shown.
- [ ] Every 15 minutes is OFF.
- [ ] No Gmail module is shown.
- [ ] No email automation is shown.
- [ ] CRM output/demo sheet is clean.
- [ ] French wording is ready if the prospect is French-speaking.
- [ ] One-page pilot offer is ready if needed.
- [ ] Reply handling document is available internally, not shown by default.

---

## 4. 15-Minute Call Flow

Suggested timing:

### Minute 0-2 — Opening

Goal:

- Set context.
- Keep the tone simple and professional.

Suggested wording:

Bonjour, merci pour votre temps.  
L’objectif aujourd’hui est simplement de vous montrer une idée courte autour du suivi des demandes entrantes.  
La démonstration reste volontairement simple et manuelle.

### Minute 2-5 — Current Process Questions

Ask:

- How do incoming requests arrive today?
- Who reviews them?
- Where are they stored?
- What information is often missing?
- Which follow-up steps are easy to forget?
- Which actions must always stay manual?

Do not diagnose too aggressively.

Do not claim that the current process is broken.

### Minute 5-9 — Manual Demo

Show only a safe manual-first demo.

Demo points:

- A request comes in.
- The request is structured.
- AI prepares an internal summary.
- A priority or category is suggested.
- A next manual action is suggested.
- A human reviews before any external communication.

Mandatory safety wording:

La validation humaine reste nécessaire avant toute communication externe.  
Il ne s’agit pas d’un système d’envoi automatique d’emails.

### Minute 9-11 — Connect Demo to Prospect Context

Explain:

- This could help organize incoming requests.
- This could reduce forgotten follow-up steps.
- This could make the next action clearer.
- This starts as a small controlled pilot.

Avoid saying:

- This will replace your CRM.
- This will automatically reply to clients.
- This will guarantee more leads.
- This will fully automate the process immediately.

### Minute 11-13 — Paid Pilot Scope

Explain a small pilot scope:

- review current contact path;
- define one controlled intake workflow;
- prepare a simple summary and next-action structure;
- test on safe examples or approved requests;
- review results manually;
- decide whether to extend or stop.

Price wording:

For a simple paid pilot around incoming requests and manual follow-up, the indicative range is 1,200-1,500 CHF, depending on confirmed scope.

The final price should not be fixed until the scope is confirmed.

### Minute 13-15 — Next Step

Choose one next step:

- send a short written recap;
- prepare a mini proposal;
- schedule a deeper workflow review;
- stop if there is no clear need.

Do not push if the prospect is not interested.

---

## 5. Demo Safety Rules

During the call:

- Do not send any email.
- Do not show automatic email sending.
- Do not promise automatic sending.
- Do not enable scheduled automation.
- Do not change Make.
- Do not change Google Sheets.
- Do not change production.
- Do not change Vercel.
- Do not change API.
- Do not change database.
- Do not use private or sensitive data.
- Keep the demo manual.
- Keep human review as mandatory.

---

## 6. Stop Conditions

Pause or stop the call if:

- private data appears on screen;
- a Gmail module appears;
- an email automation module appears;
- scheduled automation appears active;
- Make scenario is not clearly manual;
- CRM output is not clean;
- AI output contains unsupported claims;
- the prospect asks for automatic sending before safety rules are clear;
- the scope becomes too broad for a small pilot.

If a stop condition occurs:

1. Pause the demo.
2. Explain that a safety check is needed.
3. Do not improvise.
4. Continue only after review.

---

## 7. Questions to Qualify the Paid Pilot

Ask only practical questions:

- Which request type should we test first?
- Which channel matters most: form, email, phone, or website?
- Who should review the summary?
- What information is usually missing?
- What should never be automated?
- What would make the pilot useful after one week?
- What would make the pilot not worth continuing?

---

## 8. After the Call

After the call:

- [ ] Write a short internal call note.
- [ ] Log prospect interest level.
- [ ] Log questions asked.
- [ ] Log any price discussion.
- [ ] Log whether a mini proposal is needed.
- [ ] Do not mark paid pilot unless explicitly agreed.
- [ ] Do not send follow-up without manual review.
- [ ] If sending a recap, use reply handling rules.
- [ ] If preparing a proposal, use the post-call proposal template.
- [ ] If no interest, do not push.

---

## 9. Exit Criteria

Current status:

- Call flow is clear.
- Demo remains manual.
- No automatic sending is shown or promised.
- No Gmail/email automation is proposed.
- No Make/Google Sheets changes are proposed.
- No production/Vercel/API/database changes are proposed.
- Human review remains mandatory.
- Paid pilot scope is limited.
- Next step options are included.
