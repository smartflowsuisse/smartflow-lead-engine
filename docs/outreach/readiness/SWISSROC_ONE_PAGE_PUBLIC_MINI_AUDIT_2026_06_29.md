## 4. Initial Public Mini-Audit Notes

### 4.1 Website and positioning

Public observation:

Swissroc presents itself publicly as a real estate group with several visible activity areas, including investment, asset management, development, architecture, construction, planning, and sales.

Public-only note:

This observation is based only on the public website positioning. It does not make any claim about Swissroc internal organization, tools, CRM, sales process, or operational workflow.

Opportunity angle:

Because the public positioning covers several activity areas, incoming public requests may arrive with different intent types: project discussion, investment interest, construction-related inquiry, property-related inquiry, or general contact.

A small Lead & Follow-up Pilot could help structure these incoming requests before manual internal review.

### 4.2 Contact and lead capture

Public observation:

Swissroc has a visible public contact path, including contact pages, contact details, and a public contact form asking visitors to provide basic information and select the service of interest.

Public-only note:

This observation is limited to the visible public contact path. It does not claim whether Swissroc receives many leads, how fast the team replies, or how requests are handled internally.

Opportunity angle:

A lightweight internal workflow could summarize each incoming request, classify the apparent topic, identify missing information, and prepare a manual follow-up note.

### 4.3 Follow-up opportunity

Public observation:

The public contact path is suitable for a controlled manual-first follow-up workflow because requests appear to be service-oriented and may require routing or qualification.

Public-only note:

This is an external observation only. It does not imply that Swissroc has a follow-up problem.

Possible pilot:

A lightweight Lead & Follow-up Pilot could help:

- collect public contact requests into a structured internal view;
- summarize the request with AI;
- classify the request type;
- identify urgency and missing information;
- suggest a next manual action;
- keep a human review step before any client communication.

No automatic external email sending is proposed.

### 4.4 Client-facing risk

Risk:

The mini-audit must not suggest that Swissroc has a broken or weak process.

Safe framing:

Position the pilot as a small operational support layer for public inbound requests, not as a correction of Swissroc internal work.

Respectful Swiss B2B wording:

- public contact path reviewed;
- public-only observation;
- potential improvement opportunity;
- manual-first follow-up support;
- no automation of external replies without human review.

## 5. Suggested Pilot Angle

SmartFlow Suisse could propose a small manual-first Lead & Follow-up Pilot for incoming public contact requests.
